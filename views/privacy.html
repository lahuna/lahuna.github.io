Lahuna Privacy Policy

Last modified: March 24, 2014

This Privacy Policy describes what types of information Lahuna collects from you, and how Lahuna receives, uses, and shares that information when you use our website or applications (“Services” or “Lahuna”). When using any of our Services you consent to the collection, transfer, manipulation, storage, disclosure, and other uses of your information as described in this Privacy Policy.

If you have any questions or comments about this Privacy Policy, please contact us on our contact page.

Types of Information Lahuna Collects

Log Data

Log data is information stored on remote servers that is recorded, or logged, when you use our Services. We receive log data when you interact with our Services, such as when you visit our website, sign into our Services, or interact with our email.

Log data may include information about the hardware you are using to access our Services, such as the unique device identifier, device type, model, and manufacturer. It may also include information about your software such as your web browser type, operating system, and language you are using.

Lahuna may capture and log network related information such as your IP address, mobile phone number, internet service provider, or your mobile carrier. If you have enabled your computer or device to send us location information, we may log your geographic location based on the GPS coordinates of your device or the location of your internet service provider.

Log data may also include your web browsing activity such as the date and time of the activity, search query terms you used, what webpage referred you to our Services, and what pages you visited when browsing our Services. Lahuna may log data on how you interact with links across our Services, including our email notifications.

Cookies and HTML5 Local Storage

A cookie is a small data file that is stored on your computer, mobile phone, or other device when you use Lahuna Services. The next time you use our Services, the browser sends the cookie back to our server to notify us of your previous activity. This allows us to recognize you and apply any previous progress, settings, or preferences you may have had. Like cookies, HTML5 local storage is information we store on your computer or device, but gives us the flexibility to store larger amounts of data.

Most Internet browsers automatically accept cookies. You can change your browser settings to stop accepting cookies or to prompt you before accepting a cookie from the websites you visit. However, some Services may not function properly if you disable cookies.

Pixel Tags

Pixel tags are small blocks of code on Lahuna webpages that allow us to measure a user’s activities on a particular webpage. We may also use pixel tags to notify us when an email we have sent you is opened or accessed.

How Lahuna Receives Your Information

When you authorize Lahuna’s Services to connect to your third-party account on your behalf we will pull your basic information such as your name, username, email address, and mobile phone number, if applicable. We may also retrieve more detailed information about you such as your profile bio and photo. Items you have designated as public for your third-party account will also be designated as public on any Lahuna Services.

Once you have linked your third-party account to our Services, any text, posts, lists, photos, or videos (“Content”) will be shared and automatically synchronized between our Services and your third-party. All the Content will continue to be stored on your third-party’s servers, but you will be able to view, modify, or add to it using Lahuna Services if you choose.

Finally, Lahuna can receive information about you through email correspondences you have with us and indirectly through other Lahuna users posting Content about you.

How Lahuna Uses Your Information

Accessing and using your information is very important to the basic functions of our business. When you login to Lahuna, we use your username and password to authenticate who you are so that your data can remain secure. Monitoring user data allows us to protect the rights and property of Lahuna and our users. It also keeps our products, services, and integrations with other systems safe and secure.

Lahuna uses your information to customize our Services. That way we can offer you personalized content and to develop new innovative features and Services to better meet your needs. Knowing more about you also gives us the opportunity to offer relevant advertising that shows items you are more likely to be interested in.

Lahuna’s Services use your information to help you connect with other people and to allow friends and family to find you on Lahuna or on a third-party service. With your information we can make sharing and other tasks quicker and easier. We access your information when we need to keep in touch with you to let you know about new features or changes to our Services. We may also contact you to make suggestions on how to get more out of our Services or to respond to any questions or comments you may have.

We access user information in order to maintain Lahuna’s systems and for internal operations, such as troubleshooting, data analysis, testing, and research. Analyzing user data helps us measure how we are doing so we can improve our Services. It also allows us to assess the effectiveness and improve the quality of our advertising.

When Lahuna Shares Your Information

There may be cases when Lahuna shares your information with other individuals, companies, organizations, governments, or law-enforcement agencies (“Recipients”). We would do this if the access, use, preservation or disclosure (“Sharing”) of the information is reasonably necessary to comply with a law, regulation, government request, or legal process (like a search warrant, court order, or subpoena).

Lahuna may allow Sharing your information with Recipients if we have a good faith belief that it is necessary to detect, prevent, and address fraud or other illegal activity; to resolve security or technical issues; to enforce our terms of service and to investigate possible violations; or to protect against harm the rights, property, or safety of Lahuna, our users, or the public as required or permitted by law.

Should the ownership of Lahuna change, your information may be sold or transferred as part of that transaction. However, your information will remain protected as specified in this Privacy Policy.

Making Your Information Public

Our Services are designed to help you share Content with the world in a public manner. When you share Content via the Services, you should think carefully about what you are making public. Remember that when you share information publicly it is associated with you and it may show up when someone does a search on Lahuna, the third-party where the information is stored, or on a public search engine. You should only share information with people you trust because they will be able to save it or re-share it with others.

Changes to This Policy

We may periodically revise this Privacy Policy. The most recent version of the policy will dictate our use of your information and will always be on our website. If we make a change to this policy we will notify you via a website notice or email. Once you begin using the Services after the Privacy Policy changes have been posted to our website, you agree to be bound by the new Privacy Policy.
