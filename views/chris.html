Chris Davison

Principal Architect and Developer

Chris has two degrees from California State University, Long Beach. A Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering (BSEE) and a Master of Business Administration (MBA) in Finance.

While working on his Engineering degree in college, Chris began his career at Unisys writing firmware for RAID disk array controllers. At Unisys he was able to apply what he learned in school to a real-world project. His position required a combination of computer hardware and software expertise.

Chris then went back to Cal State Long Beach to get an MBA degree. Specializing in Finance and participating in the Student Managed Investment Fund (SMIF), Chris realized he had a second passion: Investing money. He then became a licensed independent Registered Representative and began managing investment portfolios for individual clients.

Chris then had an opportunity to partner up with Qualified Capital to help them grow their investment advisor services. In that position, it became apparent that Qualified Capital, and the financial services industry in general, used severely antiquated computer systems to manage their client data, run reports, and perform due diligence for the regulatory agencies. So Chris shifted back to programming and developed a software application to manage and report all aspects of the investment advisory process, such as portfolio management, asset allocation, and performance reporting.

After about a year, Washington Mutual Financial Services noticed what Chris was doing at Qualified Capital and asked him to join their development team as a consultant. At WMFS he was tasked with building an application that would support backoffice personnel from compliance to the call center. Using Microsoft web technologies with an Oracle database the application provided data entry, importing data from 3rd party web services, data transformation, and reporting.

Chris later worked as an independent consultant in the healthcare industry writing a Silverlight application used for processing medical insurance claims. It was that experience that led him to realized the power of Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) and how businesses could leverage each other in mutually beneficial ways by sharing data. How information from different sources could be brought together, processed, and sent back out by using the Internet as a conduit.

Chris' current focus now is developing responsive HTML5 web apps using AngularJS and Bootstrap.
